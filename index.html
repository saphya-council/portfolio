"宝贝"/"HATCH"

We realized that the problem with the culture of online dating is that people are less likely to meet in person, which is an issue we hope to rectify through our app.

A combination of Neopets and Tinder, users will match with each other based on common interests in their profiles, thus enacting an event where they must find and foster a pet.

These rules are put in place to establish a serious minded relationship between two people. We want users to use our app as a means for physical interaction, not to prolong virtual discourse.

Users can obtain resources in different places within the academic building to raise their pet. In the cafeteria one can scan for food; in the cafe one can scan for treats; and at any water cooler one can scan for water. The bare necessities. Users are also urged to visit their pet together every now and then to give their pet a love boost.

For our assignment on photogrammetry, I took several photos of the service elevator and imported them into Agiscan. I was confused as to why the three-sixty rendered image was blotchy, missing the important details of the environment such as the reflection of light on the metal elevator doors and the area outside the window. Also, when exported, for some reason the 3d model lacked the textures I saw when viewing them in the photogrammetry software.

Nonetheless, I wanted to see if I could somehow use what I ultimately ended up with, in my roller ball game. I imagined the elevator as a prop in a dystopian city, a product of a nuclear meltdown that destroyed civilization as we knew it. And, you (the roller ball) as a mouse navigating the ruins of the city. The combination of organic grass and artificial technology of the elevator indicates a cycle of repossession by mother nature, reclaiming her territory and emphasizing her natural superiority to manmade mechanisms.

Phase 1: Design and Conquer

Since I want my game to have a pixelated aesthetic, a lot of my textures were made in the style you will see below:

First, it started with a flower.

The game is supposed to take place in Japan. I appropriated many Japanese motifs such as the cherry blossom in the main map and sumo wrestling mat in minigame one.

Some models aren't completely original. This coin for example, I made in Blender but the texture is from a .png I found on the interweb.

The picture above is the first version of my pig. I had a lot of trouble linking limbs to the pigs body through the boolean modifier so I scrapped this pig and made the one below.

The second version of the pig also had trouble coming together, but by this point I had all the animations done and textures applied so I didn't want to make a whole other model.


Phase 2: Unified in Unity

    

My game will use spherical gravity as opposed to the regular gravity used for Unity objects. The pig needs to constantly be pulled towards the center as it moves around the spherical surface. I used the following to achieve this action:

https://github.com/SebLague/Spherical-Gravity

Minigame 1:

A simple coin collecting game. Players are in a race against time in the dark as they collect coins to get the highest score in this minigame. This was by far the easiest part of the entire game, since it didn't involve scripting physics to work.

Minigame 2:

Players try to stay grounded on a ever-tilting disk rotating on a pivot. One player can throw their opponents off balance by clicking on the screen, which instantiates random objects (coins or teacups) to throw the player off balance.

Minigame 3:

Players try to remain on the spinning bottle (controlled by another player), running in the opposite direction the bottle is spinning in to avoid falling off.


Phase 3: Embellishing

I only used royalty-free sound for my game. I used sources such as freesound.org and Kevin Macleod's personal site to find materials.


Phase 4: The Future

Besides the obvious glitches, my biggest goal for this game is to add online multiplayer support. This game is ideally for Android and iOS devices so I expect to have builds for those operating systems in the future.


Updates:

New Minigame 4 (Hot Pigtato)!

After struggling with the physics in Minigame 2 and 3,  I've decided to create another minigame with easier mechanics as I work on troubleshooting the others. I took the disk from Minigame 2 and separated it into four objects in Blender before exporting the .fbx in Unity and making animations there. I was having too much trouble with exporting the Blender animations so I decided to forego that and try out Unity animations. I thought that animating objects in Unity was much easier than in Blender. With all the animations now done, I made an array with every piece of the disk inside, so that every five seconds, up to three pieces are randomly triggered. The objective of this minigame is to outlast the timer, and avoid the other player so you don't get pushed off the platform.

Minigame 1 - Pig Grows Larger!

With every coin, the pig is scaled up by (0.025f, 0.025f, 0.025f). Whichever pig reaches (0.7,0.7,0.7) first, wins the round.

Main Menu and Modes!

Instead of jumping right into the main map, players now have the option of playing story mode (main map and randomized mini games) or minigame mode where players can select whichever minigame they like best.

Instructions!
